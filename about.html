About Black Mirror

Black Mirroris a critically acclaimed British anthology series created by Charlie Brooker. The series explores a group of standalone dramas - sharp, suspenseful, satirical tales that explore techno-paranoia. It is a contemporary reworking of “The Twilight Zone” with stories that tap into the collective unease about the modern world.

Overview

Each episode has a different cast, a different setting, even a different reality. But they’re all about the way we live now – and the way we might be living in 10 minutes’ time if we’re clumsy. The series’ episodes are standalone works, so they can be watched in any order.

Seasons

The series consists of six seasons and a standalone interactive film:

  1. Season 1:Consists of three episodes, exploring themes such as public humiliation, the loss of privacy, and the impact of technology on personal relationships.
  2. Season 2:Comprises of four episodes, delving into topics like the concept of the “digital self”, the extremes of vigilante justice, and the potential horrors of a “smart” future.
  3. Season 3:Six episodes long, this season explores the impact of social media, virtual reality, and gamification on the human condition.
  4. Season 4:This season also has six episodes, with narratives around digital consciousness, parenting in the digital age, and the human desire for immortality through technology.
  5. Season 5:Consists of three episodes, focusing on the intersection of technology with pop culture, social media, and artificial intelligence.
  6. Season 6:The latest season, with narratives around streaming culture, the impact of technology on journalism, and the concept of digital afterlife.

Interactive Film: Bandersnatch

In addition to the six seasons, Black Mirror also includes an interactive film, “Bandersnatch”. The film allows viewers to make decisions for the main character, leading to multiple possible endings.

Awards and Recognition

Black Mirror has been recognized with numerous awards and nominations, including multiple Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Television Movie and Outstanding Writing for a Limited Series, Movie, or Dramatic Special.

Behind the Scenes

The series is created and written by Charlie Brooker, and executive produced by Brooker and Annabel Jones. It is produced by the British production company Zeppotron for Endemol.
