Balck Mirror | Season 03


The third season of Black Mirror includes the episode “Nosedive”, a brilliant satire on our obsession with social media validation. The episode presents a dystopian society where people can rate each other from one to five stars for every interaction they have, impacting their socioeconomic status. This season marks a shift in the series from purely dark narratives to more nuanced explorations of technology’s impact on society. However, some may argue that this shift has led to a dilution of the series’ original premise.


  1. Nosedive: The first episode, “Nosedive”, is set in a world where people can rate each other from one to five stars for every interaction they have, which can impact their socioeconomic status1. The story revolves around Lacie (Bryce Dallas Howard), a young woman overly obsessed with her ratings. She finds an opportunity to elevate her ratings greatly and move into a more luxurious residence after being chosen by her popular childhood friend (Alice Eve) as the maid of honour for her wedding.

  2. Playtest: The second episode, “Playtest”, follows Cooper (Wyatt Russell), an American who playtests an upcoming augmented reality game in London while travelling. It is a horror game which accesses his brain and targets his fears. Hannah John-Kamen co-stars as a video game journalist Cooper meets.

  3. Shut Up and Dance: The third episode, “Shut Up and Dance”, tells the story of a teenage boy, Kenny (Alex Lawther), who is blackmailed into committing bizarre and criminal acts by anonymous hackers in possession of a video of him masturbating6. The teen is joined by a middle-aged man (Jerome Flynn), who is also being blackmailed over infidelity.

  4. San Junipero: The fourth episode, “San Junipero”, is set in a beach resort town named San Junipero, where the introverted Yorkie (Mackenzie Davis) meets the more outgoing Kelly (Gugu Mbatha-Raw)8. The town is part of a simulated reality the elderly can inhabit, even after death.

  5. Men Against Fire: The fifth episode, “Men Against Fire”, follows a soldier named Stripe (Malachi Kirby) who hunts humanoid mutants known as roaches. After a malfunctioning of his MASS, a neural implant, he discovers that these “roaches” are ordinary human beings.

  6. Hated in the Nation: The sixth and final episode of the season, “Hated in the Nation”, follows Detectives Karin Parke (Kelly Macdonald) and Blue Coulson (Faye Marsay) as they investigate a spate of deaths targeting the subjects of social media hatred, at the hands of Autonomous Drone Insects (ADIs) that have been deployed to combat environmental catastrophe as bees near extinction.
