Balck Mirror | Season 04


Season 4 of Black Mirror features diverse narratives, from the space opera “USS Callister” to the romantic “Hang the DJ”. This season showcases the series’ ability to genre-hop while still maintaining its core theme of technology’s impact on society. However, the season’s ambition sometimes outstrips its execution, with some episodes feeling more like concept-driven exercises than fully realized narratives.


  1. USS Callister: The first episode, “USS Callister”, revolves around Robert Daly, a gifted programmer and co-founder of a popular online game. He creates a Star Trek-like space adventure within the game, using his co-workers’ DNA to create sentient digital clones of them. Acting as the captain of the USS Callister starship, Daly is able to order his co-workers around, bend them to his will, and mistreat them if they get out of line.

  2. Arkangel: The second episode, “Arkangel”, follows a young woman named Martha whose boyfriend, Ash, dies in a car accident. As she mourns him, she discovers a new online service that allows her to communicate with an artificial intelligence imitating Ash. Despite the initial comfort, Martha becomes increasingly frustrated with the artificial Ash’s inability to truly replicate her deceased boyfriend.

  3. Crocodile: The third episode, “Crocodile”, tells the story of a woman named Mia who commits several murders in order to cover up her past crimes. Meanwhile, Shazia, an insurance investigator, uses a “Recaller” that can project people’s visual memories onto a screen.

  4. Hang the DJ: The fourth episode, “Hang the DJ”, is set in a world where people can rate each other from one to five stars for every interaction they have, which can impact their socioeconomic status10. The story revolves around Frank and Amy, who are matched into relationships for fixed lengths of time by an algorithm that eventually determines their life-long partner.

  5. Metalhead: The fifth episode, “Metalhead”, is a post-apocalyptic episode where a woman named Bella tries to escape from robotic “dogs” after the unexplained collapse of human society. The dogs were influenced by Boston Dynamics’ robots such as BigDog.

  6. Black Museum: The final episode of the season, “Black Museum”, is divided into three stories, told by Rolo Haynes, the owner of a remote Black Museum. He tells the visitor Nish about the backstories of exhibits, which involve his previous employment in experimental technologies.
