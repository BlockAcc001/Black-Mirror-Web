Balck Mirror | Season 02


Season 2 continues the exploration of the twisted implications of technology in society. The standout episode of this season is “Be Right Back”, which tells the story of a woman who uses an online service to communicate with an artificial intelligence imitating her deceased boyfriend. This episode beautifully captures the human longing for connection and the lengths we’re willing to go to keep our loved ones alive in our memories. However, the season as a whole lacks the consistency of its predecessor, with the other episodes failing to reach the same emotional depth.


  1. Be Right Back: This episode revolves around a young woman named Martha whose boyfriend, Ash, dies in a car accident. As she mourns him, she discovers a new online service that allows her to communicate with an artificial intelligence imitating Ash1. Starting with instant messaging, Martha uploads more videos and photos and begins to talk with the artificial Ash over the phone. Despite the initial comfort, Martha becomes increasingly frustrated with the artificial Ash’s inability to truly replicate her deceased boyfriend.

  2. White Bear: The protagonist, Victoria, wakes up with no memory of her life. She finds herself in a dystopian society where people are either voyeurs, recording her every move on their phones, or hunters trying to kill her. Victoria is helped by Jem, a woman who explains that a signal broadcast from the “White Bear” transmitter has turned most people into mindless voyeurs. The episode is a suspenseful chase as Victoria and Jem attempt to evade the hunters and reach the transmitter.

  3. The Waldo Moment: This episode follows Jamie, a failed comedian who voices a popular cartoon bear named Waldo. When TV executives decide to have Waldo run for office to generate publicity for a new series, Jamie finds himself caught up in the world of politics. Despite his initial reluctance, Jamie’s alter ego, Waldo, gains significant popularity, leading to unexpected consequences.
